My philosophy

The ideals that drive me.

Technology has without a doubt increased the productivity of the world many times over. Growing up with the explosion of the 90's internet, I was able to witness first hand how the interned developed and evolved. Sadly, within the past ten years, many of the conveniences that came with the internet have a largely ignored consequence that I feel need to be addressed soon. What I am referring to is the public's surrendering of personal information for the sake of convenience. For many people, services like Facebook, Google Docs, Youtube, Spotify, and many many more have implemented business models that involve selling off your personal data in mass quantities to whoever wants to pay for it. I believe that this indifference toward the individual's privacy is a growing problem, and the consquences are already tangible. It has already been revealed that Cambridge Analytica has been caught using data bought by Facebook to influence America's 2016 Election. It is important to understand that swaying the US election was probably not in Facebook's best interest. However, whenever a massive amount of data is collected in a population, the possibilites of what can be done with that data are endless.

My personal goal as a web designer, and future software engineer is to help create alternatives to these malicious services, that are accessible and free to the public. Every website that I build is built by myself, using non malicious software, and code that does exactly what you want it to and nothing more. It is my responsibility as a professional to offer not only quality software, but software that respects the user's privacy, and data.
